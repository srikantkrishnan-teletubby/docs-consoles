Vaccination vs. Immunization — quick reference
Vaccination recordShows the vaccine was given. Does NOT require a blood test result. All three types above qualify.
Immunization recordSame as vaccination record — these terms are interchangeable. Issued by a clinic or health authority after completing the series.
Blood test / lab resultCompletely different document — go back to Step 3 and select "Blood test" instead.

Tap the result you see on the lab report. An "A" or abnormal flag does not change the result.
1. Hep B Surface Antigen
HBsAg  ·  Hep B Surf AG  ·  HBV Ag
Tests for active infection. Positive = currently infected. Negative alone does NOT prove immunity.
—
2. Hep B Surface Antibody
anti-HBs  ·  Hep B Surf AB  ·  HBsAb
The only marker that confirms immunity. Positive (≥10 mIU/mL) = immune → ACCEPT. Negative = REJECT.
—
3. Hep B Core Antibody (tiebreaker)
anti-HBc  ·  Hep B Core Ab  ·  HBcAb
Past-exposure marker only. Positive or negative alone = REJECT — Core Antibody cannot confirm immunity on its own.
